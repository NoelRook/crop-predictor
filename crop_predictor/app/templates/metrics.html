What is Indoor Farming?

Indoor farming involves growing crops inside controlled environments such as greenhouses or vertical farms. It uses technology like LED lighting, climate control, and hydroponics to maximize crop yields while using less space and water.

Why Indoor Farming Matters

Saves Resources

Indoor farms use up to 95% less water compared to traditional farming.

Year-Round Production

Grow crops regardless of the weather or season, ensuring a steady food supply.

Fresher Produce

Locally grown crops mean fresher, healthier produce with minimal transport.

How Does Indoor Farming Work?

Hydroponics

Crops are grown in nutrient-rich water, eliminating the need for soil.

LED Lighting

Specialized lights simulate sunlight, promoting plant growth indoors.

Climate Control

Temperature, humidity, and CO2 levels are optimized for crop health.
